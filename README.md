# Educational Technology Plan 

## Purpose
Provide a practical roadmap for integrating Menifee Union School District hardware and software to improve student understanding, engagement, and measurable growth in middle‑school math and financial literacy while ensuring accessibility, safety, and continuity of instruction.

---

## Course
- **Name:** Middle School Computer Science 
- **Description:** Integrated course combining grade‑level math (ratios, proportions, integers, basic algebra, data/graphing) with applied financial literacy (budgeting, saving, interest, taxes, simple investing, decision‑making). Emphasis on project‑based learning and digital creation.  
- **Grade level:** 6–8 (adaptable; scaffold outcomes by grade)

---

## Situation
- **Assets**
  - Menifee Union School District devices and network (Chromebooks, teacher laptops, projection/interactive displays, Wi‑Fi)  
  - District Google Workspace for Education (Classroom, Drive, Forms, Sheets, Slides, Meet)  
  - Existing classroom platforms: Project STEM, Scratch  
  - Teacher experience with project‑based and computational thinking approaches
- **Constraints**
  - One Chromebook per student but occasional sharing/limited chargers  
  - Variable home internet/access for some students  
  - District privacy/content filters may restrict external sites  
  - Diverse student needs (ELs, IEP/504) requiring differentiated supports
- **Context considerations**
  - Use district accounts and approved tools only  
  - Plan for low‑bandwidth and offline options  
  - Ensure accessibility and compliance with FERPA/COPPA and district policies

---

## Software Specs
- **Student-facing**
  - Google Classroom — assignments, distribution, feedback, calendar  
  - Google Docs / Sheets / Slides — notes, budgeting spreadsheets, data analysis, presentations  
  - Google Forms — formative checks, quizzes, exit tickets  
  - Scratch — coding projects to model math/financial concepts  
  - Project STEM — interdisciplinary project modules  
  - Optional (district approval required): Desmos (graphing), Khan Academy (practice/videos), EverFi / Practical Money Skills (financial modules)
- **Instructor-facing**
  - Google Drive, Google Classroom grading, Google Forms analytics, district SIS for official records  
  - Screencast tools (Loom / Screencastify) if district‑approved
- **Compliance**
  - Use district‑approved tools only; ensure third‑party tools meet district data privacy agreements; use district logins

---

## Hardware Specs
- **Student hardware**
  - Chromebooks (one per student preferred), ChromeOS up‑to‑date; capable of running Scratch 3.0 and Google Workspace  
  - Headphones with microphone for video lessons and speech tools  
  - Access to scientific calculator app or district‑approved physical calculator for assessments
- **Teacher hardware**
  - District laptop (primary) and teacher Chromebook (backup) with HDMI / USB‑C output  
  - Projector / interactive display or large monitor; document camera; webcam for virtual lessons
- **Classroom infrastructure**
  - Reliable classroom Wi‑Fi; charging cart or charging access; external storage or district cloud backup for media

---

## Application
- **Instructional approach**
  - Blended learning, project‑based learning (PBL), computational thinking, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), formative assessment cycles
- **In-class**
  - Warm‑up: Google Form quick checks (auto‑scored)  
  - Mini‑lesson: interactive display + document camera; screencast posted in Classroom  
  - Guided practice: Chromebook activities (Desmos, Sheets simulations) in pairs  
  - Maker / PBL: Project STEM modules and Scratch projects (budget simulators, interest visualizers)  
  - Exit: Google Form or Google Doc reflection
- **Homework & long-term assignments**
  - Google Classroom assignments linking to Khan Academy practice, Sheets budgeting projects, Scratch project checkpoints  
  - Group budget portfolio (income/expenses, savings goals, investment simulation) submitted as Slides / Drive folder with spreadsheets and reflection
- **Assessment**
  - Formative: Google Forms, Project STEM / Desmos diagnostics, teacher checklists  
  - Summative: Performance rubrics for projects, unit tests via Google Forms where appropriate, portfolio reviews  
  - Differentiation: scaffolded templates, tiered tasks, peer review, IEP/504 accommodations
- **Justification**
  - Efficiency: Google Workspace automates distribution/collection and provides immediate data for adjustments  
  - Effectiveness: Active learning, PBL, and coding promote conceptual understanding and transfer to real financial contexts  
  - Inclusivity: Multimodal resources, translation and accessibility features, scaffolded templates for diverse learners

---

## Impacts
- **Student learning outcomes**
  - Improved computational fluency, ability to model financial scenarios, and applied numeric reasoning demonstrated through digital artifacts (Scratch projects, budgets, portfolios)
- **Equity & accessibility**
  - Offline / low‑bandwidth options (printable packets, in‑school lab access); Google accessibility tools (voice typing, Translate), captioned videos, alternative assessments per IEP/504
- **Resiliency & continuity**
  - Centralized content in Google Classroom; screencasts and materials ready for remote transition; contingency plans for device outages (paper backups, flexible deadlines); regular backups to district Drive
- **Legal / ethical**
  - Compliance with Menifee Union School District policies, FERPA, COPPA; parental permissions for non‑district tools; integrated digital citizenship instruction (privacy, copyright, netiquette)
- **Evaluation metrics**
  - Formative quiz pass rates, rubric scores on projects, portfolio growth, pre/post diagnostic comparisons, and student engagement surveys with quarterly review and adjustments
